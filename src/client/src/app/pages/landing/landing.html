What DDRS Does

Everything the barangay needs
to manage dog registration

From owner onboarding to vaccination records and incident reports — DDRS covers the full lifecycle of dog management at the barangay level.

Owner & Dog Registration

Register dog owners and their pets with full profiles — breed, age, sex, and address — linked to a specific purok within the barangay.

Vaccination Records

Track anti-rabies vaccinations per dog with automated due-date reminders. Generate vaccination certificates compliant with BHERT requirements.

QR Code Identification

Each registered dog gets a scannable QR ID tag containing owner info, vaccination status, and registration details — instantly verifiable in the field.

Purok Map View

An interactive barangay map displaying dog density and vaccination coverage by purok — filterable by status to surface unvaccinated dogs at a glance.

Incident Reporting

Log biting incidents and stray dog sightings with location tagging. Automatically cross-references the involved dog's vaccination history.

Reports & Analytics

Generate summary reports on registration rates, vaccination coverage, and breed distribution — ready for submission to municipal health offices.

The Process

From owner to registered dog
in four steps

BHERT staff guide residents through the process on-site, or owners can self-register online through the portal.

01
Owner Creates Account

The dog owner registers with their name, address, and purok. Identity is verified by barangay staff on first registration.

02
Dog Profile Submitted

Owner fills in the dog's details: name, breed, sex, age, and color. A unique dog ID is assigned upon submission.

03
Vaccination Logged

BHERT staff records the anti-rabies vaccination with date and vaccine batch. The system sets the next due date automatically.

04
QR Tag Issued

A printable QR ID tag is generated. The owner attaches it to the dog's collar — scannable by any authorized health worker.
